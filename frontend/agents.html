What this research is

We are running a systematic experiment to find out what predicts whether TikTok viewers stay or leave, as measured by brain response rather than editorial intuition. The channel under study is @thecybersecrets (cybersecurity content, short-form TikTok).

The core tool is TRIBE v2 (Meta FAIR, 2026) — a video brain-encoding model that predicts whole-brain fMRI BOLD responses, second-by-second, for any video. Output shape: (T, 20484) float32 cortical vertices per video at 1 TR/second. We map vertices to named brain regions via the Destrieux surface atlas (nilearn).

We cross-reference these predictions with per-second audience retention curves from TikTok Creator Analytics for the same videos.

Experiment 1 — Brain Probe (completed)

Question: Can a diffusion model — guided by a whiteboard/diagram prompt — produce images TRIBE scores higher than the source frame?

Method: extract peak-activation second from top-3 composite videos → ComfyUI img2img (DreamShaper 8, denoise=0.55, 25 steps) → loop into 6s video → TRIBE score → compare delta.

VideoSource typeOrig compositeGen compositeΔ composite
A — 7556715516782497037Cluttered phone screenshot−0.352+0.044+0.397
B — 7600984382559505695Clean whiteboard diagram+0.351+0.416+0.065
C — 7559991058684300574Dark blurry floor shot−0.046+0.504+0.550

Key finding: delta is inversely proportional to how readable the source frame already was. The whiteboard/diagram prompt acts as a gradient toward what TRIBE predicts FFA/MT+ want. FFA and MT+ both lifted in all three cases. STS didn't move (no speech in a still-image loop — expected). Script: scripts/brain_probe_pipeline.py. Results: outputs/brain_probe/probe_results.json.

Experiment 2 — Iteration Loop (completed)

Question: If you chain img2img (each output feeds the next input), does the brain score keep climbing over 4 iterations?

StepVideo A compositeVideo C composite
Source (iter 0)−0.352−0.046
Iteration 1−0.239+0.360
Iteration 2−0.150+0.427 ★
Iteration 3−0.207+0.329

Key finding: neither trajectory is monotone. Video C peaks at iter 2 then decays — the high-activation configuration is unstable under further diffusion. Blind chaining is not a reliable optimizer. The implication is beam search: generate several candidates per step, score all, keep the best.

Script: scripts/brain_probe_iterate.py. Results: outputs/brain_probe/iter_results.json.

Active work — Target-directed iteration (in progress)

The iterate loop currently hill-climbs on raw composite (scalar). We are upgrading it to vector-directed optimization toward a target activation profile from a better-performing channel (@davidbombal, cybersecurity/networking, ~350K followers).

Pipeline:

  • Step 1 (running now): scripts/sweep_target_channel.py --handle davidbombal --n 25 --cap 60 — downloads and TRIBE-scores 6 davidbombal videos (most content is >60s). Log: outputs/sweep_davidbombal_run.log.
  • Step 2 (after sweep): scripts/build_target_profile.py --handle davidbombal — computes mean FG vector from top 50% by composite → saves outputs/target_profiles/davidbombal.json.
  • Step 3: scripts/brain_probe_iterate.py --all --iters 4 --target-profile outputs/target_profiles/davidbombal.json — runs the directed iterate loop; reports both composite score and cosine similarity / L2 distance to target at each step.

Scoring change: replaces composite = FFA + MT+ + STS with cosine_similarity(fg_vector, target_fg_vector) across all 12 functional groups. Both metrics are reported. Functions are in brain_probe_iterate.py: score_toward_target(fg, target_fg) returns (cos_sim, l2_dist).

Caveats to carry forward

  • TRIBE predicts the average brain across 25 training subjects — not any individual viewer.
  • Still-image loops suppress MT+ (no optical flow) and STS (no speech). Compare deltas, not absolute scores, in probe/iterate experiments.
  • Current findings are from short clips (4–13s). Length confound is controlled by within-bin comparison.
  • Tier 2 analysis (within-video: what brain region activates 5s before a viewer drops off) needs >20s clips — unlocks as the sweep reaches longer videos.
  • Hemodynamic lag is 5s. TRIBE scores content quality with a 5s lag baked in — it doesn't score the first impression or hook.
  • denoise=0.55 in ComfyUI retains ~45% of source structure. Higher denoise → bigger delta, less faithfulness.
